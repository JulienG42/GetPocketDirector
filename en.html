Plan a shoot without scattered tools

Prepare your videos more simply with an app that brings lighting conditions, weather, shoots, equipment, checklists, and important documents together in one place.

Whether you are preparing an interview, a social media video, a short film, a report, or an event capture, Pocket Director helps you anticipate what needs to be checked before shooting.

The app displays useful shooting conditions: weather, temperature, humidity, cloud cover, sunrise and sunset, golden hour, blue hour, and white balance recommendation. Simple tips help you adapt your settings based on the light, time, and current conditions.

Pocket Director also lets you create shoots with a date, location, description, planned equipment, and shots to capture. The Preparation section helps you manage your equipment, add it to a shoot, and check what is ready before leaving.

A Documents section brings together important admin items: authorizations, image rights, permits, contracts, call sheets, or documents to print before the shoot.

Pocket Director is organized around four areas: Conditions, Shoots, Preparation, and Documents.

The Conditions tab lets you check weather, daylight, golden hour and blue hour times, as well as a white balance recommendation adapted to the place and time.

The app also displays simple tips to adjust your shooting according to clouds, time of day, color temperature, and any possible need for an ND filter.

The Shoots tab lets you create projects with a date, location, description, conditions preview, planned equipment, and shots to capture.

The Preparation tab lets you manage your equipment, add it to a shoot, create one-off items, and check what is ready before leaving.

The new Documents tab lets you gather authorizations, image rights, permits, contracts, call sheets, and useful documents before a shoot.

Quick additions make admin preparation easier with document templates to obtain, print, or process.

Pocket Director includes native macOS navigation and local data storage.

Frequently asked questions

What is Pocket Director for?

Pocket Director is a macOS app designed to help prepare independent shoots. It brings together weather conditions, daylight, golden hour and blue hour times, white balance recommendations, shoots, checklists, equipment, and useful documents before filming.

Does Pocket Director replace a cinematographer or assistant director?

No. Pocket Director does not replace human experience or artistic choices. The app provides practical reference points to better anticipate a shoot, avoid forgetting things, and make better decisions before filming.

Does the app collect personal data?

Pocket Director does not collect telemetry data. The app does not track your usage, does not include behavioral analytics tools, and does not send usage statistics to the publisher.

Some data may however be used locally to make the app work: your shoots, checklists, equipment, plans, documents, preferences, or entered locations.

Is my data sent to external servers?

Data created in the app is first saved locally on your device.

If iCloud synchronization is available and enabled, some data may be synced through iCloud / CloudKit so you can find it on your compatible Apple devices. This data remains associated with your iCloud account and is managed by Apple services.

Pocket Director does not sell your data and does not use it for advertising purposes.

Why does the app ask for location access?

Location access makes it possible to display weather and lighting conditions adapted to your position: weather, cloud cover, sunrise and sunset, golden hour, blue hour, and white balance recommendation.

You can refuse location access. In that case, you can manually search for a city or address.

Can I use Pocket Director without iCloud?

Yes. Pocket Director is designed to work locally. iCloud is only used to sync data when this feature is available. The app should remain usable even if you are not signed in to iCloud or if synchronization is temporarily unavailable.

How do I delete my data?

Data can be deleted from the app settings, through the data management option.

Complete deletion removes Pocket Director local data and may also delete data synchronized with iCloud when that synchronization is available.

How are updates installed?

For the macOS version distributed outside the App Store, Pocket Director may use an integrated update system to check whether a new version is available and offer to install it.

If the app is distributed through the App Store in the future, updates will go through Apple’s usual system.

Does Pocket Director work offline?

Yes, part of the app can work offline, including shoots, checklists, equipment, plans, and documents that have already been saved.

Some features still require an internet connection, such as retrieving weather data, searching for places, or iCloud synchronization.

Are weather and lighting recommendations guaranteed?

No. The information displayed is provided for informational purposes only. Real conditions may vary depending on the location, time, weather, environment, and equipment used.

Pocket Director helps prepare a shoot, but it is always useful to check conditions on location before filming.

Is Pocket Director intended for professionals?

Pocket Director is designed first for creators, independent videographers, students, small crews, and lightweight shoots. The goal is to provide a simple, clear tool without unnecessary complexity.

It can also be useful for more experienced profiles who want to prepare a shoot quickly without opening fifteen different tools.

Is an iPhone version planned?

Pocket Director is first developed for macOS. An iPhone version is being considered so you can access your shoots, checklists, and important information directly in the field.
